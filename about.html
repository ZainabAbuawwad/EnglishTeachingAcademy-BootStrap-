our story —

Three tutors, one classroom, a stubborn idea

In 2015, Lexicon Academy opened with three tutors, one rented classroom, and a simple belief: people don't learn to speak a language by studying it silently — they learn by talking, stumbling, and trying again.

That belief shaped everything that followed. We kept classes small on purpose. We hired tutors who could explain a grammar rule in one sentence and then get out of the way so students could actually use it. A decade later, we've grown into a full academy — but the classroom still feels like a conversation, not a lecture.

"We don't teach English. We teach people to stop being afraid of it."

what we believe —

The three rules every tutor teaches by

Speak from lesson one

You won't spend weeks on silent grammar drills. You'll be forming sentences — imperfect ones — from your very first class.
